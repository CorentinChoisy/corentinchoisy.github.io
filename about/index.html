Hello, I'm Frederick.

But you can call me Fred. Nice to meet you.

I'm an INTJ currently studying Applied Mathematics and Physics at the University of Georgia. Born on the coast of Melbourne, Florida and raised in suburbs of Atlanta, Georgia, I’m interested in a multitude of topics spanning the various disciplines of applied mathematics, physical science, consumer technology, and digital design.
Hello.
As a highly self-motivated student and maker, I’m pursuing a career under the broad applied mathematical sciences umbrella. Considered by my family and peers to be a resident techie, I enjoy staying current with consumer technology and computer UI/UX design. When I’m not at my desk crunching numbers, I enjoy playing music, cycling, and riding motorcycles.
Sitting in the Big Apple.
Besides my collegiate work, I’m currently involved with the Math Club and the Society of Physics Students chapter at UGA. I was also asked to share my thoughts about Google Glass for UGA’s student newspaper The Red & Black.

I participated in Fairfield University's Mathematics & Computational Science REU during the summer of 2014 researching Experimental and Numerical Studies of Oceanic Overflow where my group was able to present at various universities, the American Physical Society’s Division of Fluid Dynamics Conference in 2014, and the Joint Mathematics Meeting in 2015.

During the beginning of 2015 I will be co-instructing a 3D printing course for the Maker Lab at Double Helix STEAM Academy in Athens, GA.

For the past year I have been conducting undergraduate mathematics research under Dr. David Gay exploring 3D printing in topology by observing the physical geometry of shapes that are particularly hard to visualize. I have written about my research and my 3D models have sparked interest in the mathematical/3D printing community.

Computer

My primary computer is an early 2011 15" MacBook Pro with the high-resolution screen upgrade, which replaced a late 2007 iMac (yes—the white one) which I have had for nearly 8 years now. I plan to upgrade to the 15" Retina MacBook Pro once the next refresh is released.

When I'm at my desk I also use a Logitech G502 mouse and an Asus 27" 1440p monitor.

Services

If I'm using my computer I'm mostly likely listening to music on Spotify. I rely on Dropbox as my primary digital workspace. For getting things done I use Trello to organize coursework, research, and projects while occasionally creating simple lists in Wunderlist. I prefer Trello to other GTD services as it can export specific tasks to my calendar while preserving the task’s due date, time, and notes.

When browsing the internet I constantly save things to my Pocket to read whenever I get a few minutes, and I typically store long term articles and ideas in Evernote. I also use 1Password for saving passwords and Mint for tracking money. Oh, and IFTTT can be handy too.

Software

I've used Mathematica to symbolically solve multivariable calculus problems, numerically compute matrix transformations, and generate 3D models for my undergraduate research project. While Mathematica is my go-to computational tool, I keep Python and MATLAB close by for alternatives.

For 3D printing I use Mathematica and OpenSCAD to generate models, Blender for computer renders and model/mesh manipulation, and Makerware for print preparation. If you are interested in creating your own models, Tinkercad is an easy-to-use, free modeling application that runs in the browser—no downloading required!

I typeset LaTeX in TeXShop, take notes in Markdown with Mou, and write code in whatever IDE I'm testing (still haven't found my favorite).

I tried Pixelmator instead of Adobe’s CC to avoid cost, and it’s been a great tool for visual mockups and light photo editing. I also prefer Alfred to OS X’s Spotlight to open files, launch applications, and search the internet because it’s fast—really fast.

I carry Shure SE215 isolating headphones/earbuds with me at all times as they provide excellent sound on the go and never fall out of my ears.

Photography

As an amateur photographer, I'm shooting with a hand-me-down Nikon D80, but I always have my iPhone on me to snap a quick picture. Many of my pictures are macro shots of 3D printed models, so a light tent is my most used accessory.
